lanczos algorithm + iMPS

1. symbols

  • H: iMPO describes the Hamiltonian of the 1D infinite-size chains.
  • v: iMPS describes the states in the Hilbert space.
  • H(c): a scaled iMPO, where every tensor of H is timed by constant c.
  • v(c): a scaled iMPS, where every tensor of H is timed by constant c.
  • $T[iX]$: the transfer matrix for an infinite-size tensor network iX. The leading eigen-value should be unit.

2. algorithm

(a) v0: a random iMPS
(b) $|\tilde{v}_1\rangle$: a iMPS which satisfies

​ $\langle \tilde{v}_1|v_0\rangle=0$

Let’s set

$|\tilde{v}_1\rangle = H(c_0) |v_0\rangle - a_0 \vert v_0\rangle$.

We need to identify the constant $c_0$ and $a_0$ by the above condition.

$0=\langle \tilde{v}_1|v_0\rangle$

$=[ \langle v_0| H(c_0) - \langle v_0 | a_0 ] |v_0\rangle$

$= \langle v_0| H(c_0) |v_0\rangle- \langle v_0 | a_0 |v_0\rangle$

$= \langle v_0| H(c_0) |v_0\rangle- a_0 \langle v_0 |v_0\rangle$

One can see that

$a_0 = \frac{\langle v_0| H(c_0) |v_0\rangle}{ \langle v_0 |v_0\rangle}$

Furthermore, $c_0$ can be identified so that the leading eigenvalue of $T[\langle v_0| H(c_0) |v_0\rangle ]$ is unit.

Finally, we transfer $|\tilde{v}_1\rangle$ into its canonical form,

$\mathcal{C}(|\tilde{v}_1\rangle) =|v_1\rangle$.

  • Construct the transfer matrix $T[\langle \tilde{v}_1 |\tilde{v}_1\rangle]$
  • Diagonalize the transfer matrix and figure out its leading eigenvalue $\lambda_1$
  • $| v_1 \rangle = | \tilde{v}_1 (\frac{1}{\lambda_1}) \rangle $

where the latter satisfied

$|v_1\rangle = H(c_0) |v_0(\frac{1}{\lambda_1})\rangle - a_0 \vert v_0(\frac{1}{\lambda_1})\rangle$.

It is clear that

​ $\langle v_1|v_0\rangle=0$.

(c) $|\tilde{v}_2\rangle$: a iMPS which satisfies

$\langle \tilde{v}_2|v_0\rangle=0$,

$\langle \tilde{v}_2|v_1\rangle=0$.

Let’s set

$|\tilde{v}_2\rangle = H(c_1) |v_1\rangle - a_1 \vert v_1\rangle - b_1 |v_0\rangle$.

We need to identify the constant $c_1$, $a_1$ and $b_1$ by the above two conditions.

  • Firstly, let consider the condition $\langle \tilde{v}_2|v_1\rangle=0$, which leads to

$0=\langle v_1 | \tilde{v}_2 \rangle$

$= \langle v_1 | [ H(c_1) |v_1\rangle - a_1 \vert v_1\rangle - b_1 |v_0\rangle ]$

$= \langle v_1 | H(c_1) |v_1\rangle - a_1 \langle v_1 \vert v_1\rangle - b_1 \langle v_1 |v_0\rangle$

$= \langle v_1 | H(c_1) |v_1\rangle - a_1 \langle v_1 \vert v_1\rangle$

It is clear that

$a_1=\frac{ \langle v_1 | H(c_1) |v_1\rangle}{\langle v_1 \vert v_1\rangle}$.

Again, $c_1$ can be identified so that the leading eigenvalue of $T[\langle v_1| H(c_1) |v_1 \rangle ]$ is unit.

  • Secondly, we consider the condition $\langle \tilde{v}_2|v_0\rangle=0$, which leads to

    $0=\langle v_0 | \tilde{v}_2 \rangle$

    $=\langle v_0 | [ H(c_1) |v_1\rangle - a_1 \vert v_1\rangle - b_1 |v_0\rangle ]$

    $= \langle v_0 |H(c_1) |v_1\rangle - a_1 \langle v_0 \vert v_1\rangle - b_1 \langle v_0 |v_0\rangle $

    $= \langle v_0 |H(c_1) |v_1\rangle - b_1 \langle v_0 |v_0\rangle $

    Thereby, we will have

    $b_1=\frac{\langle v_0 |H(c_1) |v_1\rangle}{ \langle v_0 |v_0\rangle}$

$|v_1\rangle = H(c_0) |v_0(\frac{1}{\lambda_0})\rangle - a_0 \vert v_0(\frac{1}{\lambda_0})\rangle$.

$\langle v_0 |H(c_1) |v_1\rangle = [] |v_1\rangle$

$c_1$ can be identified so that the leading eigenvalue of $T[\langle v_0| H(c_1) |v_1\rangle ]$ is unit.
